Gianluca Galletti

I'm a PhD student in Brandstetter's group at JKU Linz (Austria), working on large-scale turbulent physical simulations with machine learning.

I got my Master's degree from TU Munich and my Bachelor's degree at the University of Bologna, my birthplace and hometown.

I have been practicing traditional archery for 5 years. I also enjoy weightlifting, car racing, hiking, and sometimes climbing.

Github /  Google Scholar  /  CV /  Email

Research

Currently working on 5-dimensional plasma turbulence modeling in Tokamaks with machine learning, and industrial domain generalization for metal processing on a variety of manufacturing conditions.
During my Master's I got involved with academic research, with some applications of Graph Neural Networks and Equivariance for particle fluid problems. In the past I've dealt with reinforcement learning, computer vision and autonomous driving.
I've also worked in industry on process mining and production chain simulation throughout my Master's.

Projects

LagrangeBench

lagrangebench

LagrangeBench is a JAX-based machine learning benchmarking suite for Lagrangian problems. It provides a variety of models, datasets and utilities to train and evaluate particle-based approaches.

pyTORCS

pyTORCS-docker

Container-based TORCS environment/interface for reinforcement learning developed in Python, following the OpenAI Gym API style. TORCS is an open-source racing game. Part of my BSc thesis.

Equivariant JAX

segnn-jax painn-jax egnn-jax

Collection of some popular equivariant models implemented with the JAX library. Performance and runtimes are validated on the original configuration and against the results from the official papers.
